# Nyawit
An animation about Sawit
Code processing using the Java programming language to create an animation. This animation's theme is oil palm (sawit).

Main SDG topic: SDG 15 Life on Land

Sysnopsys:

In a thriving tropical forest, trees live as a close-knit community — walking, talking, and holding each other up through an invisible web of intertwined roots beneath the soil. Led by the wise Old Banyan, they have never known life any other way. That is, until the Palm family arrives with legal documents in hand, claiming ownership of the land and forcing every native tree to leave.

With heavy hearts, the forest trees relocate to a hillside across the valley and slowly rebuild their life together. Meanwhile, the Palms take over the old land, planting themselves in neat, uniform rows and proudly declaring that they need no one else.

When a violent storm strikes months later, the difference becomes devastating. The palm plantation — with its shallow, isolated roots and lifeless soil — cannot hold back the flood. A massive landslide swallows the entire estate. But on the hillside, the diverse forest stands firm, its deep and interconnected roots absorbing the rain and anchoring the earth.

As the floodwaters recede, the surviving Palms climb the hill in silence. Old Banyan welcomes them — not without condition, but without bitterness. The forest grows again, messier than before, but stronger.

A forest is only as resilient as its diversity — and a community is only as strong as its willingness to hold each other.
